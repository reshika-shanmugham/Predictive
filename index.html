Formula Evaluation Workbench

1. Mechanical Power Output (P_out)

$$P_{\text{out}} = \frac{\tau \times N \times 2\pi}{60000} \text{ kW}$$

This measures the actual, useful muscle pushing your wheels forward.

  • N (Motor Speed / RPM): How fast your wheels are spinning.
  • Torque (\(\tau\)): The raw twist muscle of the motor.

Substitution Evaluation: Select a historical snapshot to see evaluation.

2. Wasted Energy Losses (P_losses)

$$P_{\text{losses}} = P_{\text{baseline}} + P_{\text{therm}} + P_{\text{drag}} + P_{\text{emi}}$$

Whenever a machine runs, the universe demands an energy tax. You never get 100% perfect power; electricity always leaks out as waste:

  • \(P_{\text{baseline}}\) (Humming Tax): The basic cost of turning the magnets on.
  • \(P_{\text{therm}}\) (Sweat Penalty): Sweat penalty over 95°C.
  • \(P_{\text{drag}}\) (Sludge Tax): Fluid drag and axle weight friction.
  • \(P_{\text{emi}}\) (Static Leak): Unshielded static noise.

Substitution Evaluation: Select a historical snapshot to see evaluation.

3. Core System Efficiency (\(\eta\))

$$\eta = \left(\frac{P_{\text{out}}}{P_{\text{out}} + P_{\text{losses}}}\right) \times 100$$

A percentage score from 0% to 100% telling you how much battery juice is actually turning your wheels versus how much is accidentally melting your engine.
